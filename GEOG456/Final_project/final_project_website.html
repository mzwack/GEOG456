Context

A Background in Women's Ice Hockey

Since women's ice hockey debuted at the 1998 Winter Olympics, the sport has grown dramatically. This growth led to the development of several women's hockey leagues beginning in 1999, and the eventual development of the contemporary Professional Women's Hockey League (PWHL) in 2023. In the following 2024/25 season, USA Hockey reached an all-time high of 577,900 total members, with female participation increasing by 5.1%. Yet recognition of how women and girls contribute to the sport continues to lag, as seen by public discussions surrounding the 2026 Milan-Cortina Olympics.

The primary data sources are USA Hockey's annual registration reports, published continuously since 2008 and publicly available through each organization's webpage. As well as Stathletes-tracked women's hockey event data from the Olympics and NCAA and fastRhockey: Functions to Access Professional Women's Hockey League and National Hockey League Play by Play Data.

Visualization 1

Registration of Women and Girls by State

This cartogram maps the geographic spread of female hockey participation across the United States, drawing on USA Hockey's annual registration reports from 2007 through 2024. Each state is represented as a bubble sized and shaded in proportion to its registration count. The cartogram style allows registration density to be immediately visible to users in a way traditional maps do not. The timeline slider allows viewers to animate three decades of growth, watching the sport's registration numbers change year by year. Toggling between total registrations and a per-100k residents adjusts for state population, exposing where women's hockey has genuinely grown versus where raw numbers simply reflect a large underlying population.

Visualization 2

PWHL Franchise Map and Player Data

The Professional Women's Hockey League launched in 2024 with 6 teams and expanded to 8 in 2025. This interactive map shows each team's home stadium location, choropleth shading by player origin (U.S. state or Canadian province), and full 2025–26 roster details.

The choropleth layer reveals a clear geographic concentration of talent — particularly in Ontario and Minnesota — that mirrors the registration growth patterns shown in Visualization 1.

  • Click any team logo marker to open a full roster popup with stats
  • Hover any state or province to see how many PWHL players come from there
  • International players are listed separately within each team popup
  • Coaching staff including GM, Head Coach, and Assistants for all 8 teams
